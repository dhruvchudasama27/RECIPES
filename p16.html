Ingredients
  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and cubed
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup tamarind chutney
  • 1/4 cup green chutney (coriander-mint chutney)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup sev (fried gram flour noodles)
  • 1/4 cup pomegranate seeds (optional, for added freshness and sweetness)
  • Chaat masala (optional, for extra flavor)
Instructions
  1. Boil and Prepare Potatoes:
    • Boil the potatoes until they are fork-tender but not mushy.
    • Let them cool, peel, and chop them into small cubes.
  2. Spice the Potatoes:
    • In a mixing bowl, add the boiled potato cubes.
    • Sprinkle red chili powder, roasted cumin powder, and salt over the potatoes.
    • Gently toss the potatoes to coat them evenly with the spices. Set aside.
  3. Prepare Chutneys:
    • Prepare the tamarind chutney and green chutney if you don't have them ready. You can find many recipes for these online or use store-bought chutneys.
    • Whisk the yogurt in a bowl until smooth. Add a pinch of salt and mix well.
  4. Assemble Aloo Chaat:
    • Place the spiced potato cubes on a serving platter.
    • Drizzle the tamarind chutney and green chutney over the potatoes according to your taste.
    • Spoon the yogurt over the potatoes as well.
  5. Garnish:
    • Sprinkle chopped red onions, green chilies, and fresh coriander leaves over the potatoes.
    • Top with sev (fried gram flour noodles) for a crunchy texture.
    • Optionally, add pomegranate seeds for a burst of sweetness and color.
    • If desired, sprinkle a little chaat masala for extra flavor.
Aloo Chaat is best enjoyed immediately.
Serve it as a snack or appetizer, and enjoy the burst of flavors!
