Ingredients
  • 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 6-8 dried red chili peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 medium-sized tomato, chopped
  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind, soaked in water
  • 2-3 cups water
  • 1/2 cup cooked and mashed toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chili peppers
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Rasam Powder:
    • Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, dried red chili peppers, fenugreek seeds, and mustard seeds until fragrant.
    • Allow them to cool, and then grind them into a fine powder along with turmeric powder. Set aside.
  2. Extract Tamarind Juice:
    • Squeeze the soaked tamarind to extract its juice. Strain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Rasam:
    • In a pot, add the chopped tomatoes, tamarind juice, water, and a pinch of asafoetida. Cook on medium heat until the tomatoes become soft and pulpy.
  4. Add Rasam Powder and Dal:
    • Add the prepared rasam powder and the cooked and mashed toor dal to the pot. Mix well to combine.
  5. Season the Rasam:
    • Add salt, curry leaves, and chopped coriander leaves. Allow the rasam to simmer for a few minutes until the flavors meld together.
  6. Prepare the Tempering:
    • In a small pan, heat ghee or oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chili peppers, and fenugreek seeds. Once the mustard seeds start spluttering, add a pinch of asafoetida. Remove from heat.
  7. Add Tempering to Rasam:
    • Pour the prepared tempering over the rasam and mix well.
Your Rasam soup is ready to be served!
