Ingredients
  • 1 cup moth beans (matki) or mixed sprouts (matki, moth, and green gram)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Misal masala (readily available spice blend) or use homemade spice blend (see below)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Finely chopped onions
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Sev (crunchy chickpea flour noodles)
  • Lemon wedges
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Pav (bread rolls)
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Homemade Misal Masala (if not using store-bought):
    • Dry roast all the whole spices mentioned for the masala in a pan until they become fragrant.
    • Allow them to cool and then grind into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Sprouted Beans:
    • Wash and rinse the sprouted beans.
    • In a pressure cooker, add the sprouted beans with enough water to cover them. Pressure cook for 1-2 whistles or until they are cooked but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  3. Make the Misal:
    • Heat oil in a pan or large skillet. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
    • Add minced ginger and garlic, and sauté for a few more minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate.
    • Add the Misal masala (or homemade spice blend), turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Sauté for a couple of minutes.
    • Add the cooked sprouted beans and stir well to coat them with the spice mixture.
    • Add water to achieve your desired consistency for the curry. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  4. Assemble Misal Pav:
    • Ladle the misal into serving bowls.
    • Top each bowl with chopped onions, fresh coriander leaves, sev, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  5. Serve with Pav:
    • Serve Misal Pav with pav (bread rolls) on the side. You can lightly toast the pav with a bit of butter if desired.
Enjoy your spicy and delicious Misal Pav!
