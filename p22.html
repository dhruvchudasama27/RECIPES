Ingredients
  • 1 liter of full-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped pistachios and almonds for garnish
Instructions
  1. Making the Rasgulla (Cottage Cheese Balls):
    • In a large heavy-bottomed pan, bring 1 liter of milk to a boil. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and add 2 tablespoons of lemon juice or vinegar. Stir continuously until the milk curdles and the whey separates. If it doesn't curdle completely, you can add a little more lemon juice or vinegar.
    • Once the milk has completely curdled, remove it from the heat. Place a muslin cloth or cheesecloth over a large strainer and strain the curdled milk. Collect the whey in a separate bowl; this can be used for other purposes.
    • Rinse the chenna (paneer) under cold water to remove any traces of lemon juice or vinegar. Squeeze out the excess water by tying the cloth and hanging it for about 30 minutes to drain.
    • After 30 minutes, transfer the drained chenna to a clean surface. Add semolina, cardamom powder, and a pinch of baking soda. Knead the mixture well until it becomes smooth and starts leaving the sides of your hand.
    • Divide the mixture into small portions and shape them into small balls. Make sure there are no cracks in the balls.
    • In a separate pot, combine 1 cup of sugar and 4 cups of water. Bring it to a boil to make a sugar syrup.
    • Add the chenna balls to the boiling sugar syrup. Cover and cook for about 20-25 minutes on medium heat. The balls will double in size and become spongy. They are ready when they start floating on the surface.
    • Turn off the heat and let the rasgullas cool down in the sugar syrup. They will absorb the syrup and become sweet.
  2. Preparing the Ras (Sweetened Milk Syrup):
    • In a separate wide pan, bring 4 cups of milk to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the milk reduces to about half its original quantity.
    • Add sugar, saffron strands (if using), and cardamom powder to the reduced milk. Stir well and simmer for another 5-7 minutes until the sugar dissolves completely.
  3. Assembling Ras Malai:
    • Take the rasgullas out of the sugar syrup and gently press each one between your palms to remove excess syrup.
    • Place the rasgullas in the sweetened milk (ras) and let them soak for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator. You can garnish with chopped pistachios and almonds.
    • Serve chilled and enjoy your homemade Ras Malai!
Ras Malai is a delightful dessert
that's perfect for special occasions and celebrations.
