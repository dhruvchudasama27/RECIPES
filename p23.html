Ingredients
  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon green chili paste (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Khandvi Batter:
    • In a mixing bowl, whisk together besan, yogurt, water, turmeric powder, green chili paste, ginger paste, and salt until you have a smooth batter without any lumps.
  2. Cooking the Khandvi Mixture:
    • Pour the batter into a non-stick pan or a heavy-bottomed pan.
    • Cook the batter on low-medium heat, stirring continuously to prevent lumps and sticking. It will thicken gradually.
  3. Testing for Consistency:
    • To check if the batter is ready, take a small spoonful of the batter and spread it thinly on the back of a greased plate. Let it cool for a minute.
    • If you can roll it easily without any cracks, it's ready. If it breaks, continue cooking for a few more minutes, and repeat the test.
  4. Making Khandvi Rolls:
    • Once the batter is ready, quickly pour it onto a clean, greased surface or a plate. Spread it thinly and evenly using a spatula before it cools down. You need to work quickly as the mixture will start setting.
  5. Cutting Khandvi Rolls:
    • After spreading the mixture, let it cool for a few minutes, but not completely. Then, cut it into long strips using a knife.
  6. Rolling Khandvi:
    • Carefully roll each strip into a cylindrical shape. Be gentle to avoid breaking them.
  7. Prepare the Tempering:
    • Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
    • Add sesame seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute until the curry leaves become crisp.
  8. Temper the Khandvi:
    • Pour the tempering over the rolled Khandvi pieces.
  9. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with grated coconut and chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Cool and Cut:
    • Let the Khandvi cool down for a bit, then slice them into bite-sized pieces.
Your homemade Khandvi is ready to serve.
Enjoy this delicious Gujarati snack with green chutney or tamarind chutney.
