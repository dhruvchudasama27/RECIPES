Mug Dal Bhajiya

Ingredients

  • 1 cup split green gram (mug dal)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Wash the split green gram (mug dal) thoroughly and soak it in enough water for about 3-4 hours or overnight. Drain the water.
  2. In a blender, add the soaked and drained green gram along with chopped green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Blend it into a coarse paste without adding too much water. You want a thick and slightly grainy consistency.
  3. Transfer the blended paste to a mixing bowl.
  4. Add finely chopped onions, cumin seeds, red chili powder, turmeric powder, asafoetida, and salt to the green gram paste. Mix everything well to combine.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai on medium-high heat.
  6. Once the oil is hot, drop small portions of the green gram mixture into the hot oil using a spoon or your fingers. Fry the bhajiya in batches. Do not overcrowd the pan.
  7. Fry the bhajiya on medium heat, turning occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crispy. This should take about 4-5 minutes per batch. Make sure they are cooked evenly.
  8. Using a slotted spoon, remove the fried bhajiya from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to remove excess oil.
  9. Continue frying the remaining batches of bhajiya.
  10. Serve the hot and crispy Mug Dal Bhajiya with green chutney or tamarind chutney and enjoy as a delicious snack or appetizer.
These Mug Dal Bhajiya are best enjoyed when they are fresh and hot.
They have a crispy exterior and a soft, flavorful interior.
Enjoy them with your favorite chutney and a cup of tea for a perfect evening snack.
