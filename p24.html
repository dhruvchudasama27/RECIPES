Ingredients
  • 1 cup fresh pigeon peas (tuvar lilva), shelled
  • 1 cup fresh beans, chopped
  • 1 cup sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup purple yam (kand), peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup small brinjals (eggplants), slit
  • 1 cup raw bananas, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup small potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (methi)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh coconut
  • 2-3 green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Masala Paste:
    • In a blender, combine all the ingredients for the masala paste and grind them into a smooth paste. Add a little water if needed. Set this paste aside.
  2. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Wash and clean all the vegetables.
    • Mix the pigeon peas, beans, sweet potatoes, purple yam, brinjals, raw bananas, and potatoes in a large mixing bowl.
    • Add the fenugreek leaves and grated coconut to the bowl. Mix them with the vegetables.
  3. Stuff the Masala:
    • Take the prepared masala paste and stuff it into the brinjals and potatoes.
  4. Cook the Undhiyu:
    • Heat oil in a large, deep, and heavy-bottomed pan or a pressure cooker.
    • Add the mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida for tempering. Let them splutter.
    • Add the stuffed vegetables and the remaining mixture of vegetables and fenugreek leaves to the pan.
    • Sprinkle salt and some extra red chili powder if you want it spicier.
    • Mix everything gently to coat the vegetables with the masala.
    • Add a little water to create steam and cover the pan. Cook on a low flame for about 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Serve:
    • Once the Undhiyu is cooked and the vegetables are tender, remove it from heat.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and grated coconut.
    • Serve hot with puris or rotis.
Enjoy your homemade Undhiyu, a delicious and hearty Gujarati dish!
