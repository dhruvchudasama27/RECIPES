Ingredients
  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (chole), soaked overnight or for at least 8 hours
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water, as needed
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
Instructions
  1. For Chole (Chickpea Curry):
    • Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas. In a pressure cooker or a large pot, cook the chickpeas with enough water and a pinch of salt until they are tender. If using a pressure cooker, it may take about 2-3 whistles, or if using a pot, it may take about 45 minutes to 1 hour. Drain and set aside.
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom pods. Sauté for a minute or until they release their aroma.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown.
    • Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
    • Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes.
    • Add the cooked chickpeas and about 1-1.5 cups of water. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the curry thickens and the flavors meld together.
    • Sprinkle garam masala and stir well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and set aside.
  2. For Bhature (Fried Bread):
    • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, yogurt, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.
    • Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a smooth, pliable dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 2 hours.
    • After resting, divide the dough into small, golf ball-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls.
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium-high heat.
    • Roll each dough ball into a thin circle using a rolling pin. The bhature should be about 6-7 inches in diameter.
    • Carefully slide the rolled bhature into the hot oil. Press gently with a spatula to puff them up. Fry until they turn golden brown on both sides, flipping as needed.
    • Remove the bhature from the oil and drain them on paper towels.
Serve the hot Chole with Bhature. You can also serve them with
pickles, sliced onions, and a side of yogurt for a complete meal. Enjoy!
