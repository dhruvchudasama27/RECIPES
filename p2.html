Ingredients
  • 1 cup split yellow lentils (moong dal or toor dal)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped, for garnishing
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Lentils:
    • Rinse the lentils thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • In a pressure cooker or a pot, combine the lentils and 4 cups of water. Cook until the lentils are soft and mushy. If using a pressure cooker, this usually takes about 2 whistles on medium heat. If using a pot, it might take around 30-40 minutes. You can also cook the lentils in an Instant Pot.
  2. Sauteed Aromatics:
    • In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add Ginger and Garlic:
    • Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Tomato and Spice Mix:
    • Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the mixture becomes a thick paste.
  5. Combine Lentils and Aromatics:
    • Add the cooked lentils to the tomato-spice mixture. Mix well.
  6. Adjust Consistency:
    • If the soup is too thick, add water to achieve your desired consistency. Dal soup is usually of medium thickness.
  7. Season and Simmer:
    • Add garam masala and let the soup simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  8. Optional Tadka (Tempering):
    • In a small pan, heat ghee over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  9. Add Tadka to Soup:
    • Carefully pour the prepared tadka over the simmering dal soup. It will infuse the soup with extra flavor.
  10. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish the dal soup with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side. Squeezing a little lemon juice into the soup just before eating enhances the flavor.
Enjoy your delicious and hearty Dal Soup!
It's great on its own or served with rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.
