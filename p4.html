Ingredients
  • 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 tablespoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 4-6 dried red chili peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)
  • 2-3 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, eggplant, drumsticks, etc.), chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 medium-sized tomato, chopped
  • Small lemon-sized tamarind ball, soaked in water
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chili peppers
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Sambar Powder:
    • Dry roast coriander seeds, chana dal, urad dal, fenugreek seeds, dried red chili peppers, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, and mustard seeds until aromatic.
    • Allow them to cool, and then grind into a fine powder along with turmeric powder. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Lentils:
    • Wash and pressure cook the toor dal until it's soft and mushy. Set aside.
  3. Extract Tamarind Juice:
    • Squeeze the soaked tamarind to extract its juice. Strain and set aside.
  4. Prepare the Sambar:
    • In a large pot, add the chopped mixed vegetables, chopped onion, and tamarind juice. Add enough water to cover the vegetables.
    • Cook the vegetables until they are tender.
  5. Add Sambar Powder and Dal:
    • Add the prepared sambar powder, turmeric powder, and cooked toor dal to the pot. Mix well.
  6. Season the Sambar:
    • Add salt, curry leaves, and chopped coriander leaves. Allow the sambar to simmer for a few minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  7. Prepare the Tempering:
    • In a small pan, heat ghee or oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chili peppers, and a pinch of asafoetida. Once the mustard seeds start spluttering, remove from heat.
  8. Add Tempering to Sambar:
    • Pour the prepared tempering over the sambar and mix well.
Your Sambar is ready to be served!
