Ingredients
  • 300 grams fresh spinach leaves (palak)
  • 2 cups water (for blanching)
  • A bowl of ice-cold water (for blanching)
  • 250 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream or cashew cream (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves, optional)
  • 2 tablespoons butter (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
Instructions
  1. Blanch the Spinach:
    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and the spinach leaves.
    • Let the spinach cook for about 2 minutes until wilted. Immediately remove the spinach and transfer it to a bowl of ice-cold water. This will help retain the bright green color.
    • Drain the spinach and blend it into a smooth puree using a blender or food processor. You can add a little water if needed to facilitate blending.
  2. Prepare the Paneer:
    • Heat a pan over medium heat and add a little oil. Add the paneer cubes and fry them until they turn golden brown on all sides. Remove the paneer cubes from the pan and set them aside.
  3. Make the Palak Paneer:
    • In the same pan, add the remaining oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
    • Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a few more minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, ground coriander, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes.
    • Add the spinach puree and mix it with the spice mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    • Add the fried paneer cubes and garam masala. Stir well and let it simmer for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
    • If using cream, add it at this stage and mix well. You can also add a little water to adjust the consistency of the gravy.
    • Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the pan. Add butter if desired for extra richness. Stir well.
    • Let the Palak Paneer simmer for a few more minutes, then remove it from heat.
  4. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish the Palak Paneer with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
Enjoy your homemade Palak Paneer, a creamy and nutritious delight!
