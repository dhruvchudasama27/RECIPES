Ingredients
  • 250 grams paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup thick yogurt (hung curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning)
  • Vegetable oil for brushing
Instructions
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, lemon juice, salt, and vegetable oil to make the marinade.
  2. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and gently toss to coat them evenly. Allow the paneer to marinate for at least 1-2 hours in the refrigerator. Longer marination imparts more flavor.
  3. If you're using vegetables, you can also marinate them in a separate bowl using a portion of the marinade.
  4. Preheat your grill or oven to 200°C (392°F). If using an oven, you can use the broil function.
  5. Thread the marinated paneer cubes and vegetables onto the soaked wooden skewers, alternating between paneer and veggies.
  6. Brush the skewers with a little vegetable oil to prevent sticking to the grill or oven grates.
  7. Place the skewers on the grill or in the oven and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the paneer and vegetables are charred and cooked through. You can baste them with extra marinade or oil for added flavor.
  8. Once the Paneer Tikka is cooked and has a nice char, remove it from the grill or oven.
  9. Serve the hot Paneer Tikka with some lemon wedges and mint-coriander chutney. You can garnish it with fresh coriander leaves and onion rings for extra flavor.
Enjoy your homemade Paneer Tikka as an appetizer or snack.
It's best served hot and fresh.
