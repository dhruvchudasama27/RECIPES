Ingredients
  • 4 cups grated carrots (about 4-5 large carrots)
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 3-4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, and pistachios) for garnish
  • Raisins for garnish (optional)
Instructions
  1. Wash, peel, and grate the carrots using a fine grater. Set aside.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai, add the grated carrots and milk. Bring them to a boil over medium heat. Stir occasionally to prevent the milk from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  3. Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and simmer the carrots and milk. Continue cooking, stirring frequently, until the milk reduces and thickens. This process can take about 30-40 minutes.
  4. Add the sugar and mix well. The sugar will release moisture, so you'll need to cook it until the moisture evaporates and the halwa thickens again.
  5. In a separate small pan, heat the ghee. Add the chopped nuts and saut√© until they turn golden brown. You can also add raisins at this point and fry them until they plump up. Set aside.
  6. Continue cooking the carrot mixture, stirring regularly until it reduces further and starts leaving the sides of the pan. This can take another 15-20 minutes.
  7. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using). Mix well for a minute to infuse the flavors.
  8. Add the fried nuts and raisins to the carrot halwa. Reserve some for garnishing.
  9. Cook for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Taste and adjust the sugar if needed.
  10. Remove the Gajar Halwa from the heat and transfer it to a serving dish.
  11. Garnish with the reserved nuts and serve hot or at room temperature.
Enjoy your homemade Gajar Halwa!
