Ingredients
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-green chili paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons Eno fruit salt or 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for greasing)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
Instructions
  1. Prepare Dhokla Batter:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, chickpea flour, yogurt, turmeric powder, ginger-green chili paste, sugar, and salt.
    • Gradually add water while stirring to form a smooth, lump-free batter. The batter should be of pouring consistency.
    • Let the batter rest for about 15-20 minutes to allow it to ferment slightly. During this time, the batter will become a bit frothy.
  2. Steam Dhokla:
    • Grease a steaming tray or a round plate that fits inside your steamer with oil.
    • Just before steaming, add Eno fruit salt or baking soda to the batter and mix well. You will notice the batter becoming frothy immediately.
    • Pour the batter into the greased tray or plate.
    • Place the tray/plate inside a steamer and steam for about 15-20 minutes on medium heat or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Be sure not to overcook, as dhokla should remain soft and spongy.
  3. Prepare Tempering:
    • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a small pan.
    • Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
    • Add asafoetida, curry leaves, and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute or until the curry leaves become crispy.
  4. Assemble and Serve:
    • Once the dhokla is steamed and cooled slightly, cut it into square or diamond-shaped pieces in the tray.
    • Drizzle the prepared tempering mixture evenly over the dhokla pieces.
    • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves, grated coconut (if using), and a sprinkle of lemon juice.
    • Allow the dhokla to absorb the tempering for a few minutes before serving.
Enjoy your homemade dhokla as a snack or a light meal.
It's a delicious and healthy option for a quick bite.
