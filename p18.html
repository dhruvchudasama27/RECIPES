Ingredients
  • 1 cup dried kidney beans (rajma)
  • 3 cups water (for soaking)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 cup long-grain Basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
Instructions
  1. For Cooking Rajma:
    • Wash the dried kidney beans (rajma) thoroughly and soak them in 3 cups of water overnight or for at least 6-8 hours. This will help them soften.
    • After soaking, drain the water from the kidney beans and rinse them once again.
    • In a pressure cooker or large pot, add the soaked and drained kidney beans along with enough water to cover them. Pressure cook for about 15-20 minutes until the rajma becomes tender. If using a pot, it may take longer, so cook until they are soft but not mushy.
    • Once the rajma is cooked, heat oil or ghee in a separate pan or skillet. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
    • Add the spices: turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
    • Add the cooked rajma to the tomato-onion mixture and mix everything well. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally, allowing the flavors to meld together.
    • If the gravy seems too thick, you can add some water to achieve your desired consistency.
  2. For Cooking Rice:
    • Rinse the Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • In a separate pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add salt and the rinsed rice.
    • Cook the rice on medium heat with the lid partially covered until it's tender and the water is absorbed, usually about 15-20 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork once it's done.
Enjoy your homemade Rajma Chawal!
