Ingredients
  • 4 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Lemon juice (from half a lemon)
Instructions
  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Wash and chop the tomatoes, coriander leaves, onion, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Sauteed Aromatics:
    • Heat the oil or ghee in a pot over medium heat.
    • Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  3. Add Ginger and Garlic:
    • Add the grated ginger and minced garlic. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Tomato and Spice Mix:
    • Add the chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, ground coriander, and a bit of salt. Mix well.
  5. Cook Tomatoes:
    • Cook the tomatoes until they become soft and start to break down. This will take about 8-10 minutes.
  6. Blending
    • Once the tomatoes are cooked and cooled slightly, transfer the mixture to a blender.
    • Blend until you get a smooth puree. Be careful while blending hot liquids - you can blend in batches if needed.
  7. Straining (Optional):
    • If you prefer a smoother texture, strain the pureed mixture through a fine mesh strainer to remove any coarse bits.
  8. Return to Pot:
    • Pour the strained mixture back into the pot.
  9. Add Broth:
    • Add the vegetable or chicken broth to the pot. You can adjust the amount based on your desired soup consistency.
  10. Season and Simmer:
    • Add garam masala and freshly ground black pepper. Adjust salt if needed.
    • Bring the soup to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  11. Add Coriander:
    • Stir in the chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Finish and Serve:
    • Turn off the heat and add a squeeze of lemon juice. Stir well.
    • Taste and adjust the seasoning, if required.
  13. Serve:
    • Ladle the Tamatar Dhania Shorba into serving bowls.
    • Garnish with a few fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with some crusty bread or naan.
Enjoy your flavorful Tamatar Dhania Shorba soup!
