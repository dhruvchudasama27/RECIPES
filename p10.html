Ingredients
  • 1 cup regular rice (parboiled or idli rice)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking and grinding
  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnish
Instructions
  1. For Preparing Dosa Batter:
    • Wash the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately, and soak them in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
    • Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them to a smooth batter using enough water. The batter should have a slightly thick pouring consistency, similar to pancake batter. Add salt and mix well.
    • Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume. The fermentation time may vary depending on the climate and temperature.
  2. For Preparing Potato Filling (Masala):
    • Heat oil in a pan or skillet. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.
    • Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
    • Add turmeric powder and mix well.
    • Add the mashed potatoes and salt to the pan. Mix everything thoroughly, ensuring the potatoes are well coated with the spices. Cook for a few minutes until the filling is heated through. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and set it aside.
  3. For Making Masala Dosas:
    • Heat a non-stick dosa pan or a well-seasoned cast-iron skillet on medium-high heat.
    • Grease the pan with a little oil or ghee.
    • Pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the hot pan. Use the back of the ladle to spread the batter in a circular motion, starting from the center and working your way outwards, to make a thin crepe.
    • Drizzle a little oil around the edges of the dosa and on the top.
    • Cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crisp. The edges should lift easily with a spatula.
    • Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the dosa and fold it in half or roll it up like a cylinder.
    • Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
Enjoy your homemade Masala Dosa!
